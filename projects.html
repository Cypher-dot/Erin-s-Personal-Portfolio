Analysis of BoE Speeches & UK Economic Trends

Timeline: 2023-2024

As part of a certication course at the London School of Economics and Political Science, our team conducted a project analyzing the impact of Bank of England (BoE) speeches on UK economic trends over the past three decades. The primary focus was to determine whether the sentiment conveyed in these speeches could effectively mitigate economic shocks. Using Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques, we processed and cleaned the speech data, combining it with economic indicators. I was in charge of data collection, ensuring that all datasets were accurate and ready for analysis. Through a combination of exploratory visualizations and statistical correlations, we evaluated how speech sentiment inuenced both public perception and economic stability. In my role, I also led the writing of the nal report and conducted the detailed analysis to ensure the project adhered to its scope. The nal insights revealed patterns in the BoEâ€™s soft inuence, such as how "uncertainty" sentiments were linked to economic instability and the proactive approach taken during inationary periods. The project concluded that while BoE communications played a role in managing public sentiment, there was more to explore, especially regarding how voting unity in the Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) aligned with public sentiment. This project underscored the value of central bank communications in shaping economic outcomes.
